### 1. BANANA

- **Selection:** No green at stem (unless for cooking). Small brown "sugar spots" indicate peak sweetness.
- **Technique A (Fresh): BrÃ»lÃ©e.**
  - Slice lengthwise. Sprinkle sugar. Torch until glassy crack. Warm, custardy interior.
- **Technique B (Preserved): One-Ingredient Ice Cream.**
  - Freeze ripe slices. Blend in food processor. The pectin content makes it creamy like soft-serve without adding dairy.
- **Technique C (Waste): Peel Bacon.**
  - Marinate peels in soy/smoke. Pan fry. Vegan umami snack (popular in zero-waste kitchens).

### 2. MANZANA (Apple - Red/Green)

- **Selection:** Hard as a rock. If it yields to pressure, it's mealy.
- **Technique A (Fresh): Compressed Apple.**
  - Vacuum seal slices with elderflower syrup. The pressure breaks cell walls, making it translucent and dense like juicy glass.
- **Technique B (Cooked): Tarte Tatin Style.**
  - Caramelize slowly in butter and sugar until mahogany. Pectin sets the caramel into a jelly.
- **Technique C (Waste): Apple Scrap Vinegar.**
  - Cores + Peels + Water + Sugar. Ferment 2 weeks. Homemade cider vinegar.

### 3. PERA (Pear)

- **Selection:** Check neck. If neck yields slightly, it's ripe. Body stays hard.
- **Technique A (Fresh): Poached in Wine.**
  - Classic. Red wine + star anise + cinnamon. Keeps texture firm while absorbing color.
- **Technique B (Texture): Crisp/Chip.**
  - Mandoline thin slices through the core (star pattern). Dehydrate. Beautiful edible garnish.
- **Technique C (Waste): Pear Honey.**
  - Boil cores and skins for juice. Reduce juice with sugar until honey consistency.

### 4. LIMÃ“N (Lemon/Lime)

- **Selection:** Heavy for size = Juice. Smooth skin = Thin pith (better).
- **Technique A (Service): Supreme Cut (Vifes).**
  - Remove skin and pith. Cut segments between membranes. Pure jewel of juice without pith bitterness.
- **Technique B (Flavor): Charred Lemon.**
  - Halve. Sear cut side in black skillet. Caramelizes sugars, lowers acid perception. Squeeze over fish.
- **Technique C (Waste): Salted Lemons (Preserved).**
  - Pack whole lemons in salt jar for 3 months. Skin becomes soft/edible perfume for tagines/stews.

### 5. NARANJA (Orange)

- **Technique A (Fresh): Carpaccio.**
  - Slice thin rounds. Dress with olive oil, rosemary, and sea salt. Use as savory salad base.
- **Technique B (Cooked): Gastrique.**
  - Caramelize sugar -> Deglaze with Vinegar + Orange Juice. Sweet-sour sauce for Duck/Pork.
- **Technique C (Waste): Orangette.**
  - Candied peels dipped in chocolate. Classic petit four.

## ðŸ¥­ TROPICAL & EXOTIC (Tropicales)

### 6. PIÃ‘A (Pineapple)

- **Selection:** Pull a leaf from center. If it comes out easy, it's ripe. Smell the base (butt).
- **Technique A (Fresh): Carpaccio Macerated.**
  - Paper thin slices. Drizzle with lime + chili + mint syrup. The acid "cooks" it slightly (ceviche style).
- **Technique B (Cooked): Roasted Rotisserie.**
  - Whole peeled pineapple roasted on spit with rum/brown sugar glaze. Carved like meat.
- **Technique C (Waste): Tepache.**
  - Ferment skins with piloncillo. Probiotic soda. (See Waste Catalog).

### 7. MBURUCUYÃ (Passion Fruit)

- **Selection:** Wrinkled skin = Sweetest. Smooth skin = Sour.
- **Technique A (Fresh): Caviar.**
  - Scoop out seeds. Serve over panna cotta or cheesecake. The crunch vs smooth texture.
- **Technique B (Preserved): Curd.**
  - Juice + Eggs + Butter + Sugar. Cook slowly. Rich, tart yellow butter.
- **Technique C (Waste): Shell Jam.**
  - The thick white pith of the shell is rich in pectin. Boil with sugar to make jam (remove purple skin).

### 8. AGUACATE (Avocado)

- **Selection:** Remove "button" stem. Green underneath = Ripe. Brown = Overripe.
- **Technique A (Fresh): Rose/Fan.**
  - Knife skills. Thin slices fanned out perfectly.
- **Technique B (Cooked): Grilled.**
  - Halve, stone out. Brush oil. Grill mark. Fill cavity with salsa/crab. Smoky creamy flavor.
- **Technique C (Waste): Seed Dye.**
  - Boil the seed to make a pink/orange natural fabric dye (or ink). (Not for eating).

### 9. MAMÃ“N (Papaya)

- **Techinuqe A (Fresh): Lime Cure.**
  - Papaya has a "vomit" enzyme smell (papain) to some. Squeeze generous LIME juice. It neutralizes the enzyme instantly.
- **Technique B (Waste): Green Papaya Salad.**
  - If you buy it unripe (Green), julienne it. Spicy Thai Som Tum salad. Crunchy like cucumber.

## ðŸ‡ STONE & BERRY (Carozos y Berries)

### 10. FRUTILLA (Strawberry)

- **Selection:** Red all the way to stem. Smell is #1 indicator.
- **Technique A (Fresh): Maceration 101.**
  - Toss with 10% sugar + tiny pinch of pepper/balsamic. Wait 20 mins. Syrupy red juice forms naturally.
- **Technique B (Preserved): Roast.**
  - Roast old berries. Flavor concentrates. Serve with ice cream.
- **Technique C (Waste): Top Water.**
  - Infuse the green tops in water. Makes a refreshing cucumber-like spa water.

### 11. SANDÃA (Watermelon)

- **Technique A (Texture): Pizza/Steak.**
  - Thick slice. Grill perfectly. The sugars mark beautifully. Savory treatment (Feta/Mint).
- **Technique B (Waste): Rind Pickles.**
  - Peel green skin. Dice the white rind. Pickle in sweet brine. Crunchy like cucumber.

### 12. UVA (Grape)

- **Technique A (Cooked): Roasted Grapes.**
  - Roast bunches with thyme/oil. Serve alongside duck or cheese board. They become jam bombs.
- **Technique B (Preserved): Frozen Snacks.**
  - Freeze whole. Eat like mini sorbet balls.

### 13. CIRUELA/DURAZNO (Plum/Peach)

- **Technique A (Fresh): Poached Whole.**
  - Skin on. Poach in syrup. Skin slips off, flesh is velvety.
- **Technique B (Cooked): Grilling.**
  - Halve. Remove stone. Grill mark. Serve with Burrata (Salad) or Ice Cream (Dessert).

### 14. MANGO (Rosa / Tomy)

- **Context:** In season (Dec-Feb), they are everywhere. The "Rosa" is fibrous (juice only); "Tomy" is fleshy (eating).
- **Technique A (Green/Unripe): The Street Snack.**
  - Slice unripe green mango. Toss with lime, salt, and cumin/chili. Crunchy, sour, savory.
- **Technique B (Ripe): Mango Curd.**
  - Puree flesh. Cook with lime, yolk, sugar, butter. Tropical replacement for Lemon Curd.
- **Technique C (Waste): Chutney.**
  - Use the fibrous parts (Rosa). Simmer with vinegar, onions, ginger, and raisins until jammy. Goes with pork.

### 15. GUAYABA (Guava)

- **Selection:** Fragrance is key. If it doesn't smell like perfume 1 meter away, it's not ready. Yellow skin.
- **Technique A (Classic): Cascos de Guayaba.**
  - Halve. Scoop seeds. Poach the "shells" (cascos) in vanilla syrup until tender. Serve with cream cheese.
- **Technique B (Sauce): Guava BBQ.**
  - Cook whole with onions/garlic/vinegar. Blend -> Strain (remove hard seeds). Glaze for ribs.
- **Technique C (Waste): Seed Tea.**
  - Boil the seedy pulp (removed from cascos). Strain. It makes a pink, floral iced tea.
